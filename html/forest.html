Forest Fire Prevention

Did you know?

  • Almost 40% of Bulgaria's territory is covered by forests.
  • A mature beech tree produces enough oxygen for 10 people each year.
  • A grassy area of 35 sq. m. can produce enough oxygen for one person's daily needs for a week.
  • Over 90% of forest fires are caused by or related to human activity.
  • Human negligence is the most common cause of forest fires.
  • The average speed of wildfire spread is 22.5 km/h.

How to Protect Forests from Fire

  • Do not light fires in the forest and do not use fireworks or other pyrotechnic products.
  • Keep lighters and matches away from children.
  • Never throw away unextinguished cigarettes or matches!
  • Clearing of waste in forest areas should be done by collecting and transporting to designated places. Burning is prohibited!
  • If you notice a fire, call 112 immediately and provide accurate information about your location and observations.

If You're Hiking in the Mountains

  • Only light fires in designated and secured places.
  • Prepare water or soil in advance to extinguish the fire.
  • Clear dry vegetation within at least 3 meters of the fireplace.
  • Keep the fire pit small (under 50 cm in diameter). Avoid flammable liquids.
  • Do not light fires in dry and windy conditions.
  • Never leave a fire unattended. Douse it thoroughly and check for embers.

Safety Recommendations

Understanding Smoke Signals

  • Smoke color indicates fire type and location: white = grass, dark = dense vegetation.
  • Burning debris can cause secondary fires at a distance.
  • More smoke = larger fire. Move away quickly if smoke increases.

Escape Routes

  • Follow the opposite direction of smoke tilt – that’s where the fire is heading.
  • Wind drives fire — move against it if safe.
  • If surrounded by smoke, evacuate and protect airways with wet cloth.
  • Avoid climbing uphill unless the ridge is nearby and passable.

Safe Areas

  • Head to open, vegetation-free areas or near water bodies.
  • Protect clothing from igniting. If on fire — drop, cover face, and roll.
  • Move through already burned zones if necessary, but watch for falling trees and hot ash.
