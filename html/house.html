Fire Safety Guidelines

How to React During a Fire

  • Stay calm! Panic makes it harder to respond correctly.
  • Try to put out the fire only if it's small and you have both a fire extinguisher and the skills to use it.
  • Don't use water on electrical fires or burning oil/grease!
  • If the fire is growing or threatening you, leave your home immediately.
  • Close doors behind you to limit the fire's spread.
  • Never use elevators or jump from windows.
  • If you can't evacuate, seal the door with wet clothes and signal for help from a window or balcony.
  • Call 112 and provide your exact location.
  • If there's smoke, stay low to the floor and cover your nose and mouth with a damp cloth.
  • If your clothes catch fire – stop, drop, and roll.
  • Once you've left your home – don't go back in!
  • Tell firefighters if there are people in danger or hazardous materials inside.

How to Reduce Fire Risks

In the Kitchen

  • Keep stoves and ovens clean
  • Keep flammable materials away from heat
  • Never cook when sleepy or impaired
  • Don't leave cooking unattended
  • Use timers and wear safe clothing
  • Turn off appliances when done!

Electrical Safety

  • Use proper fuses and check amperage
  • Regularly inspect wiring, plugs and outlets
  • Don't overload outlets or rely on power strips
  • Don't run cables under carpets or furniture

Electric Heaters

  • Only use working heaters without exposed elements
  • Keep them away from flammable materials
  • Never leave them unattended
  • Don't extinguish electrical fires with water!

Solid/Liquid Fuel Heating

  • Install at safe distances on non-flammable surfaces
  • Clean stoves and chimneys regularly
  • Never use gasoline or other flammable liquids to start fires
  • Don't dispose of hot ashes in plastic bins!
  • Ventilate frequently and use carbon monoxide detectors

Gas Heating

  • Don't store gas cylinders in basements
  • Ventilate rooms regularly
  • Only refill cylinders at licensed facilities
  • Watch for gas leaks – smells like garlic!
  • If you suspect a leak, close the valve, ventilate, and don't use electrical appliances!

Fireworks Safety

  • Buy only certified products
  • Use outdoors away from flammable materials
  • Keep away from children

Candles and Cigarettes

  • Never leave candles unattended
  • Place them securely, away from flammable objects
  • Don't smoke in bed!

During Holidays

  • Use only properly working decorations and lighting
  • Keep decorations away from heaters
  • Unplug everything before going to bed or leaving home

Additional Tips

  • Create an evacuation plan and practice with your family
  • Prepare an emergency bag with essential items
  • Keep keys in an easily accessible place
  • Teach children not to play with matches and lighters
  • Keep a fire extinguisher at home and learn how to use it
